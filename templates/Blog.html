7 Book recommendations to improve your Investing & Finance knowledge


1. The Intelligent Investor by Benjamin Graham

Covers key concepts like ‚Äúmargin of safety,‚Äù distinguishing investing vs speculation. Great for building a strong foundation in how to think about stock valuation and risk.

2. A Random Walk Down WallStreet by Burton G Malkiel

Introduces the idea that markets are largely efficient and that buying broad indices often makes sense. Very good for beginners who want to understand whether active stock-picking or passive investing is better. Helpful to challenge conventional investing wisdom.

3. The Little Book of Common Sense Investing by John C Bogle

A succinct, accessible book advocating for low-cost index fund investing. Especially useful if you want to understand long-term investing with minimal fuss. Emphasises fees, diversification, and staying the course.

4. The Psychology of Money by Morgan Housel

Focuses on behavioural aspects of finance: how people think about money, risk, wealth. Great for understanding the emotional & psychological side of investing (often overlooked). Recommended for all levels ‚Äî not super technical, highly readable.

5. One Up on WallStreet by Peter Lynch

A classic growth-stock investing book: look for opportunities in companies you understand. Excellent for moving beyond basics into how to pick stocks (though still with foundational discipline). Useful for retail investors who have time and interest to dig into company research.

6. Investing for Growth by Terry Smith

A more modern investing book (UK-focused) emphasising cash generation, quality businesses and long-term thinking. Good for investors based in UK / Europe or those wanting a fresh perspective beyond the US-centric classics.

7. Unknown Market Wizards by Jack D. Schwager

Offers insight into traders and investment practitioners with strong results but less notoriety. Great if you‚Äôre curious about the practical side of investing ‚Äî dealing with risk, trading, psychology, edge.
